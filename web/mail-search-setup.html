Email Search Setup

openmud can search your emails and open them in Mail.app. This uses your Mac's built-in Mail app—no iCloud API or separate login required.

Requirements

  • macOS with Mail.app
  • Mail.app with at least one email account added

Setup Steps

1. Open Mail.app

Launch Mail from Applications or Spotlight.

2. Add Your Email Accounts

  1. Go to Mail → Settings (or Mail → Preferences on older macOS)
  2. Click the Accounts tab
  3. Click the + button to add an account
  4. Choose your provider:
    • iCloud – Sign in with your Apple ID. Mail will sync your iCloud mail.
    • Google – Sign in with your Gmail. Grant access when prompted.
    • Exchange / Microsoft 365 – Enter your work email and credentials.
    • Other – For IMAP/POP3 (e.g., custom domains)

3. Let Mail Sync

After adding an account, Mail will download your emails. Wait for the initial sync to complete (check the status in the bottom of the Mail window).

4. Use Email Search in openmud

Once Mail has synced, you can ask mud1 things like:

  • "Find the email from Granite about material and aggregate pricing"
  • "Search my email for invoices from PlanHub"
  • "Find emails from City of Salt Lake about the bid"
  • "Look up emails from last week about change orders"

openmud will search your Mail database and show results. Click Open in Mail on any result to open that email directly in Mail.app.

How It Works

openmud reads from Mail.app's local SQLite database. Your emails stay on your Mac—nothing is sent to openmud's servers. Search runs entirely on your machine.

Troubleshooting

"Mail database not found" – Make sure Mail.app is installed and you've added at least one account. Open Mail and let it sync.

"No emails found" – Try broader search terms.

"Open in Mail" doesn't work – Ensure Mail.app is installed and try opening Mail manually first.
